The French Way of San Francisco

Hello and welcome to San Francisco!

This website offers a free online guided walking tour of San Francisco's surprisingly rich French history, as well as the considerable French presence today.

The History

Left Panel

The history of many of San Francisco's other immigrant communities are much better known, but the story of the city is incomplete without the contribution of the French.

On the tour you will discover that the story is not just limited to influences directly from France but from other French speaking groups as well.

This site is currently under construction and more information will be available soon. In the future the site will also feature this information in audio.

The tour begins at Union Square, just north of Market Street. The Powell Street and Montgomery Street BART stations are the closest.

Founded in 1856 after the large influx of French for the 1848-49 Gold Rush. The original building was later destroyed in the 1906 earthquake and great fire, then rebuilt in 1915.

The city of San Francisco designated the church a historic landmark in 1984.

Services are offered in French, English, and Latin.

The church has also operated a French school since 1924.

Standing at the intersection of Bush & Kearny streets you are in the heart of the French quarter, but it certain will not look like it at first glance. Like the French themselves their presence in the city is very understated and discreet. To discover the multiple restaurants which represent a variety of French regional cuisines, you’ll need to scour the alleys… literally. Highlighted on the map are Claude Lane, Hardie Place, and Belden Place, where you can discover these hidden bijous.
